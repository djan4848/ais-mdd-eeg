## 1. Comparative Signal-Trace & Parameter Sensitivity

Through our forensic pipeline re-execution across available Healthy Control samples, we evaluated the core differences destabilizing the FOOOF (SpecParam) model:

### A. Epoch Length & Concatenation (The Root Cause)
- **Old Pipeline (`cross_dataset_aperiodic_1f.py`)**: Utilized `.flatten()` on all ICA trial sources. This concatenated all epochs into a single pseudo-continuous time series comprising hundreds of seconds of data. 
- **Current Pipeline (`v3.0`)**: Employs an extremely restrictive temporal crop (`T_LENGTH = 2.0` seconds) **AND** isolates only the first trial (`ica_sources[0, ...]`) to simulate a "stationary test".
- **Impact**: SciPy’s `welch` method requires multiple overlapping segments to estimate a stable Power Spectral Density (PSD). A single 2.0-second array evaluated with `nperseg=2*fs` produces exactly **one segment**, eliminating the variance-reduction property of Welch's method. This creates a noisy, highly irregular PSD that the linear 1/f model cannot fit smoothly, collapsing the $R^2$ to ~0.30 - 0.50. 

### B. Frequency Range ([4, 40] Hz vs. [2, 40] Hz)
- Removing the Delta band (2-4 Hz) further destabilized the $R^2$. Delta frequencies hold the highest power in the 1/f distribution. Their presence "anchors" the upper limit of the aperiodic slope. Without them, the fitting algorithm is highly sensitive to isolated Alpha/Theta peaks, which warp the aperiodic exponent trajectory.

### C. ICA Label Threshold (`> 0.70`)
- The new strict threshold (`ICLabel > 0.70`) successfully purifies the signal but occasionally leads to the fallback selection of suboptimal components if the true dominant DMN source is computationally scored at 0.65. However, testing shows this alone does **not** collapse the $R^2$ below 0.90 if the temporal window is sufficiently long.
